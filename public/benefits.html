BENEFITS OF LANDSCAPING

Nature is a crucial part of our lives, but in today's busy, modernized world we are gradually distancing ourselves from it, replacing it bit by bit with bricks, concrete, and asphalt. We think that gardens are too costly and take too much effort to maintain, and it's easy just to swap out a lawn that needs mowing every few weeks, with a pavement that you can give a sweep whenever it's convenient. There are however plenty of benefits to a lush well-kept garden, and plenty of ways in which professional landscaping services can help beautify your environment without breaking the bank.

1. INCREASES PROPERTY VALUE

Good landscaping can increase your property's resale value by 15-20%. Not only that but a beautiful yard makes a home seem friendlier and more inviting to prospective buyers and can motivate a quicker sale. Businesses also benefit from this by providing clients with well-kept greenery in and outside their buildings.

2. COOLS YOUR ENVIRONMENT

Grass is much cooler than cement, bricks, asphalt, or even bare soil surfaces, and can show a difference of up to 10 degrees Celsius on a hot day. Trees are the best way to provide shade, and strategic placement can lower the temperatures within your home itself, significantly reducing expenses on air-conditioning.

3. IMPROVES WATER AND AIR QUALITY

It's a well-known fact that plants absorb carbon dioxide and produce breathable oxygen, but they also filter out other pollutants, and trap dust and smoke particles. Lawns prevent soil erosion that could filter into and pollute other water sources, and an environment consisting of vertical barriers like trees and hedges are vital to reducing noise pollution.

4. REDUCES IRRIGATION BILL

Xeriscaping is a form of landscaping that's designed to use naturally-occurring, indigenous plants, in order to reduce the need for irrigation, require minimal maintenance, in order to create a garden that's sustainable throughout the year. Other landscaping practises can utilise stormwater runoff for irrigation and allow excess water to reabsorb back into the soil.

5. PREVENTS EROSION

Soil erosion happens when rain, wind, and animals, systematically wear away and depletes the soil levels in your garden. There are plenty of landscaping practices aimed to curtail this. Physical barriers like walls and curbs prevent soil from washing away, and grass and plants protects against wind and animals. Mulching and gravel can improve drainage while retaining moisture.

6. PROTECTS NATURAL DIVERSITY AND WILDLIFE

Creating a garden filled with indigenous plants supports the local ecosystem through providing a safe habitat for various species of animals and insects. Well-landscaped areas can protect plants against extreme weather and ensuring that plant life remains healthy and is sustained over time.

7. IMPROVES WELL-BEING

The health benefits of green space are well-researched. Studies show that just looking at plants, even through a window, can lower your blood pressure and stress levels. Spending time in nature supports the immune system and can improve short-term memory. Mental health benefits include the reduction of anxious and depressive symptoms, and increases in self-esteem.
